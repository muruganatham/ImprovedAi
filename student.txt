--- Q14: explain what is a linked list? ---
Steps: 1 | Time: 15811ms
Response:
A **linked list** is a linear data structure where elements (called **nodes**) are connected via pointers. Unlike arrays, linked lists don’t require contiguous memory and can grow or shrink dynamically. This makes them efficient for frequent **insertions** and **deletions**, especially at the beginning or middle, but slower for random access. A common use is in implementing **stacks, queues**, or browser history navigation.
